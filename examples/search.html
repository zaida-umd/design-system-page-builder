AI Summary

Yes, the University of Maryland (UMD) is currently test-optional.

Here's what that means for applicants:

  • Policy Extension: UMD has extended its test-optional policy through the Spring and Fall 2027 application cycles. This means that students applying for admission between now and Fall 2027 are not required to submit standardized test scores (SAT/ACT).
  • Applicant Choice: Students have the option to decide whether or not to include SAT/ACT scores with their application.
  • Indication on Application: You will be able to indicate your test-optional status directly on your application.
  • Final Decision: Once you submit your application, your response to the test-optional question is final and cannot be changed. If you submit scores but indicate you want your application evaluated without them, the Admission Committee will not consider those scores.
  • Holistic Review: For students who choose not to submit test scores, UMD's Admission Committee uses a holistic, full-file review process. This review considers various factors, including:
    • Grades earned in academic coursework
    • Trends in your grades
    • Performance in honors, International Baccalaureate, and Advanced Placement (A/AS level) courses
    • Letters of recommendation
    • Your essay
    • Extracurricular activities and other achievements
  • No Disadvantage: A decision not to submit SAT or ACT scores will not harm a student's admission chances.
  • Impact on Other Programs: The test-optional review extends to:
    • Merit scholarships
    • Living-learning and other special programs
    • Limited Enrollment Programs (LEPs)
  • Superscoring: If you choose to submit scores, UMD superscores both the SAT and ACT, meaning they use the highest subscores from each test in their review.
  • Writing Portion and Subject Tests: UMD does not require scores from the writing sections of the SAT or ACT, nor do they require subject tests. Even if sent, these scores are not among the 24 factors reviewed for admission.
  • English Language Proficiency: Standardized tests for English language proficiency (such as Duolingo English Test, IELTS, and TOEFL) are still required for non-native English speakers.

For Transfer Students: Traditionally, transfer students with fewer than 30 semester or 45 quarter hours at the time of application would need to submit SAT/ACT scores. However, UMD's current test-optional policy applies to them as well, allowing them to choose whether or not to have their test scores considered.

Future of the Policy: UMD initially implemented its test-optional policy due to the limitations of testing during the COVID-19 pandemic. The university is currently assessing the impact of this policy on students and the admission process to inform its standardized test score requirement for application cycles beyond Fall 2027.

Clarifying Follow-up Questions: To provide the most relevant information, could you please clarify if you are applying as a:

  • Freshman applicant or a transfer applicant?
  • If a transfer applicant, how many college credits will you have completed by the time of application?

For the most current and complete information, please refer to the Office of Undergraduate Admissions.
