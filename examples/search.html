AI Summary

The University of Maryland's undergraduate admissions process is holistic, considering academic preparation alongside personal achievements and context. Most admitted first-year students rank in the top quarter of their high school class and complete a rigorous curriculum that includes honors, AP, IB, or dual-enrollment coursework when available. The middle 50% of admitted students typically present unweighted GPAs in the 3.7–4.0 range; weighted GPAs vary widely depending on the school's grading scale. Standardized testing remains optional through the current admissions cycle — applicants who choose to submit scores generally present SAT totals between 1340 and 1500 or ACT composites between 30 and 34. Beyond grades and scores, the admissions committee weighs the strength of essays, extracurricular leadership, work and family responsibilities, and demonstrated interest in your intended major. Maryland residents, first-generation students, and applicants from underrepresented regions are reviewed in the context of opportunities available to them. Final decisions reflect the full picture of an applicant's preparation, character, and potential to thrive at UMD.
